Welcome to the unofficial domain for the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C). Here you will find information about the W3C; its history, World Wide Web certification and the people behind it.

A Brief Introduction

The World Wide Web Consortium, commonly abbreviated as the W3C, is an international organisation responsible for the standardisation and advancement of the World Wide Web (WWW or simply 'the Web').

Headed by Web co-author Tim Berners-Lee, the W3C consists of 377 member organisations, administering Web development protocols and guidelines for website development. This is seperated by two categories Hypertext Markup Language (HTML) and Cascading Style Sheets (CSS) that make up the structural and aesthetic functions of the Web, and are jointly administrated and revised by the W3C.

It is the unprecedented growth of the internet, and continued evolution of ideas and technologies that have expanded the role of the W3C as it pursues its greatest challenge and ultimate goal to create and continue to improve the standards for which Web content can be built, styled and distributed.

The W3C has a major role to play in helping to continue to guide the development of the WWW and involve all peoples in its continued evolution, to allow the Web to grow and advance to reach its true potential.
