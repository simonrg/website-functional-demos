The World Wide Web Consortium, May 2011

Countries

The W3C is hosted by three major universities; MIT, INRIA and Keio University. As of 2009 the W3C also has offices in eighteen countries including Australia, Austria, Belgium, Brazil, China, Finland, Germany, Greece, Hong Kong, Hungary, Ireland, Italy, India, Israel, Luxembourg, Morocco, Netherlands, South Korea, South Africa, Spain, Sweden and the United Kingdom.

Membership in to the W3C is available to the public. Members include universities, businesses, government agencies, non-profit organisations and individuals. The role of membership in the W3C is open and transparent - anybody can join the W3C through successful application. As with any non-government organisation, the W3C is dependent upon the contribution of members and individuals who want to help contribute to the continued development and direction of the Web.

The World Wide Web Consortium (W3C) Domain is an unofficial publication containing information on the history, role and responsibilites of the W3C.
